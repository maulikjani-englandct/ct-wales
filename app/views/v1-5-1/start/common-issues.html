Common issues we are asked about

This page is also available in Welsh (Cymraeg).

Council Tax can be confusing. These are some of the common questions we get asked about:

Why is a smaller home in a higher band?

Council Tax bands are based on the price a property would have sold for on 1 April 2003. For example, a smaller property such as a flat in a premium city centre location, may be worth more than a larger home in a different area.

My neighbour extended their property but their band has not changed

If a property has been extended or improved by the current owner, the band will not be reviewed by the Valuation Office Agency (VOA) until the property is sold. For example, a neighbour has added a large extension to their house, but their Council Tax band has stayed the same, because they are still living in it. If they sell their property in the future, the band will be reviewed and may go up.

Why do Council Tax bands stay the same when house prices change?

Bands in Wales are based on property values on 1 April 2003. This means that even if house prices go up or down, Council Tax bands will stay the same.

My Council Tax bill is too high

Your Local Council is responsible for setting the amount of Council Tax you pay, not the VOA. If you think your property is in the wrong Council Tax band, you can ask the VOA to review it. You may be asked to provide evidence to support your request.

Why should I update my property details if I think they are wrong?

The VOA are responsible for keeping a fair and accurate list of all properties in Wales. If you tell us that you think your property details are wrong, it will help us to keep an accurate record for your property.
